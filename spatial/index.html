TRACK 2 · SPATIAL TRANSCRIPTOMICS

空間轉錄組學

在保留組織空間結構的前提下測量基因表達——回答「哪個細胞，在哪裡，做什麼，跟誰對話」。

Measure gene expression while preserving tissue architecture — "which cell, where, doing what, talking to whom?"

為什麼重要

為什麼需要空間資訊？

2009 年 Tang 等人的第一篇單細胞 RNA-seq 論文震撼了生物學界——我們終於能逐一細胞讀取轉錄組。但代價是：細胞必須先被酶解、懸浮、單獨封裝，所有的「位置資訊」都被沖洗掉了。對於以下問題，scRNA-seq 完全束手無策：

  • 腫瘤的侵犯前緣（invasive front）與核心區域有何不同？
  • 大腦皮層的六層神經元如何在 D-V 軸上垂直排列？
  • 肝小葉的中央－門脈軸（zonation）如何決定代謝分區？
  • 胚胎發育中，形態發生素梯度如何驅動細胞命運？
  • T 細胞是否真的與其感受到的腫瘤細胞「物理接觸」？

2016 年，瑞典皇家理工學院的 Ståhl 等人Ståhl PL et al. Science 2016: Visualization and analysis of gene expression in tissue sections by spatial transcriptomics. 這篇 Visium 前身的論文開啟了商業化空間轉錄組時代。 發表了第一個商業可行的空間轉錄組學技術（後來成為 10x Visium）。從此，空間組學成為 Nature 連續三年（2020, 2021, 2022）的「Method of the Year」候選之一，2020 年正式獲獎。

The 2009 first single-cell RNA-seq paper (Tang et al.) shocked biology — finally, we could read transcriptomes one cell at a time. But there was a price: cells must be enzymatically dissociated, suspended, and individually encapsulated, and all spatial information is washed away. scRNA-seq is helpless against questions like:

  • How does a tumor's invasive front differ from its core?
  • How are the six cortical layers stacked along the dorsal-ventral axis?
  • How does liver zonation (central-portal axis) drive metabolic compartmentalization?
  • How do morphogen gradients drive cell-fate decisions in development?
  • Is a T cell actually in physical contact with the tumor cell it senses?

In 2016, Ståhl et al.Ståhl PL et al. Science 2016: Visualization and analysis of gene expression in tissue sections by spatial transcriptomics — the paper that birthed what became 10x Visium. at KTH Stockholm published the first commercially viable spatial transcriptomics technology (the ancestor of 10x Visium). Since then, spatial omics was a Method-of-the-Year nominee three years in a row (2020-2022) and won in 2020.

比喻：拼圖 vs 散落的拼圖塊

scRNA-seq 就像把一幅 1000 片拼圖打散倒進袋子裡，然後逐片描述顏色、形狀——細節豐富，但你永遠不知道這片是「天空」還是「樹葉」，因為它們可能都是藍色的。空間轉錄組學 則是拍照同時記錄每片的座標，你可以同時知道每片「長什麼樣」和「在哪裡」。

scRNA-seq is like dumping a 1000-piece jigsaw into a bag and describing each piece in detail — rich color and shape information, but you'll never know whether a blue piece is "sky" or "blue leaf". Spatial transcriptomics photographs the puzzle in place, recording every piece's coordinates: now you know both what each piece looks like and where it sits.

比喻二：城市地圖 vs 街頭訪談

想像你在研究一座城市的就業結構。scRNA-seq 像隨機在火車站抽 10,000 個人問職業——你會得到精確的職業分佈，但無法知道金融業聚集在哪、住宅區與商業區如何銜接。空間轉錄組學 則是整個城市的「人口－職業熱力圖」——你能直接看到華爾街、矽谷、唐人街的分布；缺點是每個格子裡的個人細節不如逐個訪談清楚。結合兩者（spatial + scRNA reference）才能同時擁有解析度和地圖。

Imagine studying a city's job structure. scRNA-seq is like randomly sampling 10,000 commuters at the train station — accurate job distribution, but you can't see where finance clusters, or how residential meets commercial. Spatial transcriptomics is a city-wide heatmap of "population × profession" — you can see Wall Street, Silicon Valley, Chinatown directly; but the individual detail per pixel is fuzzier. Combining the two (spatial + scRNA reference) gives both resolution and the map.

兩大技術家族

兩大技術家族鳥瞰

空間轉錄組學技術可分為兩大家族——它們的權衡是這個領域最重要的二分法。理解這個分歧，你就理解了大半個 spatial omics。

Spatial transcriptomics splits into two technology families — this trade-off is the field's most important dichotomy. Grasp it and you've grasped half of spatial omics.

🟢 Array-based / NGS

邏輯：玻片上預先排列數百萬個帶barcode每個 barcode 對應一個唯一空間座標——讀到這個 barcode 就知道訊號來自玻片哪個 spot。 的 poly-T 寡核苷酸，組織釋放的 polyA mRNA 落在哪個 spot 就被該 spot 的 barcode 標記。

Logic: millions of barcodedEach barcode maps uniquely to one spatial position — reading the barcode tells you which spot on the slide the signal came from. poly-T oligos pre-arranged on a slide; polyA mRNA released from tissue is captured by whichever spot it lands on.

  • 優：全轉錄組（無 panel 限制），流程接近 scRNA-seq
  • 限：解析度受 barcode 大小限制（10-100 μm）
  • 代表：Visium、Visium HD、Slide-seq、Stereo-seq、HDST
  • Pro: whole transcriptome (no panel limit), workflow close to scRNA-seq
  • Limit: resolution capped by barcode size (10–100 μm)
  • Examples: Visium, Visium HD, Slide-seq, Stereo-seq, HDST
VS

🟣 Imaging-based / smFISH

邏輯：針對預設的基因 panel 設計多輪 FISH 探針，逐輪拍照——每個基因有獨一無二的「組合編碼如 MERFISH 用 16-bit Hamming 編碼：每基因在 16 輪中固定 4 輪「亮」、12 輪「暗」，靠這個 fingerprint 解碼。」，根據點在哪幾輪亮起來就能解碼是哪個基因。

Logic: design multi-round FISH probes for a pre-selected gene panel, image round-by-round — each gene has a unique combinatorial codee.g., MERFISH uses 16-bit Hamming codes — each gene "lights up" in a fixed 4 of 16 rounds; this fingerprint decodes identity. across rounds.

  • 優：亞細胞解析度，保留原位形貌，可同時看 RNA + 蛋白
  • 限：panel 多為 100-1000 基因，價格高、影像處理重
  • 代表：MERFISH、Xenium、CosMx、STARmap、seqFISH+
  • Pro: sub-cellular resolution, preserves morphology, can co-detect protein
  • Limit: 100–1000 gene panel, expensive, image-processing heavy
  • Examples: MERFISH, Xenium, CosMx, STARmap, seqFISH+

核心概念

入門前必懂的四件事

📏

解析度 vs 通量

Visium spot ~55 μm = 1-10 細胞，但量測全 ~20,000 基因。MERFISH 是亞細胞，但 panel ≤ 1,000 基因。Stereo-seq 兼顧亞細胞與全轉錄組——代價是資料量爆炸（單樣本可達 TB 級）。沒有「全勝」平台。

A Visium spot ~55 μm = 1-10 cells but measures all ~20,000 genes. MERFISH is sub-cellular but panel ≤ 1,000 genes. Stereo-seq does both but data balloons to TB scale per sample. No "win-all" platform exists.

🧪

影像 ✕ 表達

空間資料 ≠ 純表達矩陣——一定還含 H&E 或螢光組織學影像。優秀的分析會用影像來輔助分群（如 SpaGCN）、定位（fiducial alignment）、與品質檢核（壞死區辨識）。

Spatial data ≠ pure matrix — there's always H&E or fluorescent histology. Great analyses use the image for clustering (e.g., SpaGCN), alignment (fiducials), and QC (identifying necrotic zones).

🏷️

多細胞 spot

在 array-based 平台，一個 spot 不是「一個細胞」而是「附近所有細胞釋放 mRNA 的混合物」。直接做 cluster 容易得到「免疫＋上皮」混雜的奇怪 cluster。解法：deconvolution 拆比例或匹配 scRNA reference 註釋。

On array platforms, a spot is not "one cell" but "a soup of mRNA from nearby cells". Plain clustering yields odd "immune + epithelial" mixtures. Solution: deconvolution for proportions or reference-guided annotation.

🗺️

空間統計

忽略座標只看表達，你做的不是「空間」分析。真正利用空間的指標是自相關（Moran's I、Geary's C）與鄰居圖。本軌道將反覆強調這個概念。

Ignoring coordinates and just looking at expression isn't "spatial" analysis. The real tools are autocorrelation (Moran's I, Geary's C) and neighbor graphs — recurring themes in this track.
